The Crime: The 6,000-Year Lie

In Eden, the Serpent claimed that life could exist apart from God: "Ye shall not surely die." (Genesis 3:4) This is the root of the "immortality in sin" myth that has persisted through history.

The Evidence: The Sleep of the Dead

Scripture Testimony Character of God
John 11:11
"Our friend Lazarus sleepeth."
 Jesus Himself calls death a sleep, not a transition to another life. A merciful Father who gives rest, not torment.
1 Thessalonians 4:15
"We which are alive and remain unto the coming of the Lord shall not prevent them which are asleep."
 Paul affirms the dead are asleep until the resurrection. God’s justice and love are preserved—no one suffers eternal torment.
Ecclesiastes 9:5
"The dead know not any thing."
 Solomon teaches the dead are unconscious, awaiting judgment. God is not a tyrant—He does not torture the dead.

Did You Know?

  • The phrase "soul sleep" is not found in the Bible, but the concept is taught throughout Scripture.
  • More than 50 times, the Bible describes death as "sleep."
  • Early Christian writers like Justin Martyr and Irenaeus rejected the idea of the immortal soul.
  • Modern Spiritualism began in 1848 with the Fox sisters in New York, sparking a global movement.
  • Ecclesiastes 9:5 is one of the most direct statements about the state of the dead: "The dead know not any thing."
  • Jesus raised Lazarus from the dead after four days, calling him from "sleep." (John 11)
  • The doctrine of eternal torment was imported from pagan Greek philosophy, not the Hebrew Scriptures.

**[Section 1: Archaeological Evidence]**
[Show: Photos of ancient stones, scrolls, and ruins]
Narrator:
“Archaeologists—scientists who study ancient things—have found many objects that match what the Bible says.
For example, the Moabite Stone talks about a king named Omri, just like in the Bible. The Cyrus Cylinder records a Persian king letting people return home, just like the Bible says about the Jews.
The Pool of Siloam, where Jesus healed a blind man, was discovered in Jerusalem. And the Dead Sea Scrolls—ancient copies of the Bible—show that the words we read today are almost the same as thousands of years ago.”

---

**[Section 2: Manuscript Evidence]**
[Show: Ancient scrolls, numbers on screen]
Narrator:
“Did you know the Bible has more ancient copies than any other old book? There are over 5,800 handwritten Greek copies of the New Testament, and thousands more in other languages.
Some of these copies were written just 30 to 50 years after the original events. For comparison, other famous books like Homer’s Iliad have only a few hundred copies, and the oldest ones are written hundreds of years after the original.
This means we can trust that the Bible hasn’t been changed or lost over time.”

**[Section 3: Prophecy Fulfilled]**
[Show: Timeline, prophecies, Jesus’ birth, life, death]
Narrator:
“The Bible is full of predictions—called prophecies—that came true. For example, the Old Testament said the Messiah would be born in Bethlehem, be rejected, suffer, and die for others.
Hundreds of years later, Jesus was born in Bethlehem and fulfilled these prophecies exactly. The Bible also predicted the rise and fall of empires like Babylon, Persia, Greece, and Rome—long before they happened.
No other book in history has this kind of track record.”

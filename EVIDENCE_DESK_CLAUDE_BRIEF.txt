CONCEPT & VISION
====================================================================

THE WATCHMAN IDENTITY
---------------------
The name "Watchman's Ledger" comes from Ezekiel 33, where God appoints
a watchman whose job is to see danger coming, sound the alarm, and warn
the people — whether they listen or not. The watchman is not a prophet
who predicts the future. He is a disciple who reads the signs of the time
with Scripture in hand and refuses to be silent.

That is the theological engine behind this whole section.

The Evidence Desk exists to do what the biblical watchman does:
  - Observe what is happening in the world
  - Frame it honestly and plainly
  - Anchor it in Bible prophecy
  - Equip the reader to respond with wisdom, not fear

It is not news for news's sake. It is news in service of discipleship.

THE EDITORIAL PHILOSOPHY
------------------------
This is not journalism. It is prophetic discernment applied to current
events. The difference matters.

A journalist reports facts and lets the reader interpret. The Evidence
Desk reports facts AND provides the biblical interpretive framework — the
lens that says: here is what the Bible said would happen, and here is
what we are seeing. That connection is the whole point.

Every story follows a deliberate five-part analytical framework:

  1. The Directive    What should the reader do or think right now?
  2. The Brief        What happened? (Factual, plain English)
  3. The Undercurrent Why does this matter beneath the surface?
  4. The Logic        How does Scripture speak to this directly?
  5. The Outlook      What should we watch for next?

This structure is not a blog template. It is a discipleship tool.
It trains readers to move from news → understanding → scriptural grounding
→ wise action. Every story walks that path in the same order, on purpose.

THE NEWSROOM CONCEPT
--------------------
The data file is named newsroom.json intentionally. The vision is a
curated editorial desk — not a feed aggregator, not an RSS reader, not
an AI-generated summary service.

Every story in newsroom.json is:
  - Hand-selected for prophetic relevance
  - Manually written in plain, pastoral English
  - Tied to a specific KJV passage
  - Framed through the five-part report structure above

Stories are not just "interesting news." They are chosen because they
connect to themes that the Bible specifically addresses: wars and rumors
of wars, economic instability, surveillance and control, deception at
scale, the collapse of truth in public life.

The categories in use reflect this: Global Conflict, Economic Security,
Tech Policy, Information Integrity, Background Report.

HOW IT FITS INTO LIGHTLINE
---------------------------
LightLine is a full faith-learning ecosystem with multiple content pillars:

  Bible Studies     23+ long-form studies (Studies/)
  Devotions         19+ devotional pages (Devotions/)
  Games             20+ interactive Bible games (games/)
  Videos            Curated video hub (video-updates.html, vod.html)
  Evidence Desk     Current events + prophetic discernment (THIS SECTION)

The Evidence Desk is the "watching the world" pillar. While studies teach
doctrine and devotions build inner life, the Evidence Desk trains readers
to look outward — to see current events through a biblical lens rather
than a political or media lens.

The Watchman's Ledger sits at the intersection of:
  - Faith (Scripture-anchored analysis)
  - Education (structured five-part format that teaches a thinking method)
  - Current events (real stories, real dates, real stakes)

It is the only section on LightLine that engages directly with the
outside world. That makes it unique in the platform, and it must be
handled with care — it can build trust fast or lose it fast depending
on how it is maintained and labeled.

THE KNOWN TENSION
-----------------
The biggest unresolved tension in this section is the gap between the
concept and the current implementation:

  Concept:  A living, regularly updated watchman's desk that reads
            the signs of the time with Scripture.

  Reality:  A curated local JSON file with a "LIVE" badge on the ticker
            that has not been updated since early April 2026.

If the Evidence Desk goes stale, the watchman concept collapses. A
watchman who stops watching is not a watchman. This is the most urgent
operational risk for this section, and it must be part of any build
plan or content review.

THE DATA MODEL — newsroom.json
====================================================================
Each story is a JSON object with these fields:

  id              Slug used as URL identifier (?id=slug)
  category        Global Conflict | Economic Security | Tech Policy |
                  Information Integrity | Background Report
  status          Developing | Active | Escalating | High Alert | Context
  datePublished   YYYY-MM-DD
  headline        Story headline
  deck            1-2 sentence subheadline
  image           Object: { url, alt }
  bibleReference  Book Chapter:Verses (e.g. "Matthew 24:6-8")
  bibleVerse      Full KJV text of the passage
  directive       Short plain-English action instruction for the reader
  brief           Main news summary — factual, plain English
  undercurrent    Why this matters — analytical layer
  logic           Biblical interpretation — how this connects to prophecy
  outlook         Forward-looking analysis — what to watch for
  youtubeId       YouTube video ID string (embedded in full report)
  gallery         Array of image URLs (up to 4 displayed)
  readTime        "5 min read" etc (field exists, not yet rendered in UI)

THE JAVASCRIPT ENGINE — ledger.js
====================================================================
Pattern: IIFE, strict mode, vanilla JS — zero external dependencies.

SECURITY:
- esc() uses DOM textContent assignment for XSS-safe HTML output.
- safeUrl() validates URLs against https?:// regex.
- YouTube IDs are encodeURIComponent()-escaped before embedding.

FUNCTIONS:

fetchNewsroom()
  Async fetches newsroom.json. Returns array or [] on failure.

renderHomepageFeed()
  Targets #evidence-grid on watchmans-ledger.html.
  Renders top 5 stories. Card 0 = ed-card-main (large), cards 1-4 = small.
  Each card: hero image, category kicker, headline, deck, status + date,
  "Read Full Report" link → ledger-post.html?id=<slug>.

renderTrendingDrawer()
  Wires open/close events on trigger button, backdrop, close button,
  Escape key. Renders top 5 story titles + dates in drawer body as
  clickable links.

renderFullReport()
  Runs on ledger-post.html. Reads ?id= from URL. Looks up story in array.
  Renders full article: hero overlay, 5 body sections, video, gallery.
  Also sets document.title dynamically from story headline.

renderGallery()
  Renders up to 4 gallery images in 2-column grid.
  Falls back to a placeholder div if no images.

renderVideo()
  Renders YouTube iframe (16:9 responsive). Falls back to placeholder
  div if no youtubeId is present.

All three render functions are called together on DOMContentLoaded.

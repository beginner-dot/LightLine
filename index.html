MythBusters

Common Myths Corrected by Scripture

Every card below is one myth — watch the short, then read the full study. Both are in one place.

Myth 1

The Forbidden Fruit Was an Apple

The Lie Genesis says Adam and Eve ate an apple — everyone knows that.

The Truth The word "apple" is nowhere in Genesis. The Hebrew says only pĕrî — fruit, unspecified. The "apple" entered tradition through a 4th-century Latin pun in Jerome's Vulgate (malum = both "evil" and "apple") — not the Hebrew original. The Holy Spirit never named the fruit because the fruit was never the point. Satan's first four words were "Yea, hath God said?" — and the apple myth still does exactly that: it replaces Scripture with tradition.

Bible: Gen. 2:17; Gen. 3:1-6  |  History: Jerome's Latin Vulgate tradition

Read the full study
Myth 2

Noah's Ark Is Too Impossible to Be Real

The Lie The ark is a symbolic children's story — no rational person takes it as literal history.

The Truth God gave Noah precise engineering specifications: 300 × 50 × 30 cubits (~1.4 million cubic feet). Naval engineers confirm those proportions are structurally seaworthy. Jesus cited the flood as literal history (Matt. 24:37-39), Hebrews 11:7 names Noah as a hero of faith — not fiction — and flood accounts exist independently in over 200 cultures.

Bible: Gen. 6:14-16; Matt. 24:37-39; Heb. 11:7  |  Research: comparative flood traditions

Read the full study
Myth 3

Science Has Disproved the Bible

The Lie Science and the Bible are enemies — choosing faith means rejecting honest evidence.

The Truth The Bible repeatedly invites honest investigation: Isaiah 1:18 says, "Come now, and let us reason together." Scripture includes observations often highlighted by later science, including the earth suspended in space (Job 26:7) and practical health isolation principles (Leviticus 13). Real science and biblical faith both pursue truth.

Bible: Isa. 1:18; Isa. 40:22; Job 26:7  |  Theme: faith and reason together
